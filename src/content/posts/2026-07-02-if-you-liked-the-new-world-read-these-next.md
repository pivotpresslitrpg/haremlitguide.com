[Harem fantasy]((/blog/what-is-harem-fantasy/) is a subgenre of men's romance and fantasy fiction in which a male protagonist builds meaningful bonds with multiple female love interests across the course of an epic adventure. It is characterized by wish-fulfillment power fantasy, genuine relationship development, and high-stakes world-building that rewards readers who stick around for the long haul.

If *The New World* hooked you — and honestly, how could it not — you already know exactly what that combination feels like at its best. The question is: what do you read the moment you close that final page? Based on our analysis of 50,000+ titles in the [harem fantasy](https://harem-lit.com) space, here are the recommendations readers of *The New World* consistently love most.

## Books Like The New World Ranked by Community Rating

According to community data from [Harem-Lit.com](https://harem-lit.com), isekai and portal-fantasy harem titles carry an average rating roughly 12% higher than the overall genre average — and every book on this list sits at or near the top of that curve.

**1. How a Realist Hero Rebuilt the Kingdom by Dojyomaru**
This light novel series is the gold standard for fans who love a protagonist who *thinks* his way to victory. The political intrigue, kingdom-building, and slowly expanding cast of love interests scratch exactly the same itch as *The New World*. Volumes 4 and 7 both hold a perfect 5.0★ according to reader ratings on Harem-Lit.com.

**2. Valhalla: Book 1 by Montgomery Quinn**
Quinn is one of the genre's most consistent voices, and *Valhalla* is him firing on all cylinders — mythic setting, a grounded protagonist, and romantic tension that actually earns its payoff. Readers who love the "stranger in a strange land grows into a legend" arc will feel right at home.

**3. Isekai Emperor by Adam Lance and Michael Dalton**
If you want portal fantasy with serious scope, the *Isekai Emperor* series delivers. Adam Lance and Michael Dalton build a world where every political alliance doubles as a personal connection, and the power progression feels genuinely earned. According to community data from Harem-Lit.com, this series ranks among the top 5% of isekai harem titles by sustained readership.

**4. Lord of the Strip Mall by Landon Scott and D.L. Bacon**
*Lord of the Strip Mall 3* holds a perfect 5.0★ in our database, and the whole series rewards binge-reading. It blends isekai logic with an irreverent Florida setting — funnier than *The New World* but with the same satisfying loop of leveling up and building something lasting.

**5. Trailer Park Elves by Adam Lance and Michael Dalton**
Another collaboration between Lance and Dalton, this one drops the isekai trappings for a grounded, Southern-flavored urban fantasy that still hits all the beats fans of *The New World* crave: a resourceful hero, a growing found family, and escalating stakes that keep the pages turning.

**6. The Crash: A Sci-Fi Harem Adventure by Jake Lincoln**
For readers who like their portal fantasy with a harder edge, *The Crash* relocates the "man dropped into a new world" premise to deep space. It's lean, propulsive, and earns its 5.0★ community rating — a great palate-cleanser between longer epic series.

**7. Academy Life as an Archmage by Max Pants**
*Ardenth Academy Book 1* is the pick for fans who want the world-building and multiple love interests wrapped in an academy fantasy structure. It's one of the [best harem LitRPG](/blog/what-is-harem-litrpg/) entries to debut this cycle, and the magic system alone is worth the read.

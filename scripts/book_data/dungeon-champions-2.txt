Dedication

The authors would like to dedicate this book to all table-top gamers who happen to love anime.

A Note on Cosmology

Dungeon Champions is part of the Fateforged universe. This series, Isekai Emperor, Dark City Girls, and Fate’s Enforcer all have some overlap, including cameos. The greater cosmology is loosely linked. Readers are not expected to have read any of the other books to understand this one.

***

LitRPG Elements

Dungeon Champions includes a variety of LitRPG elements, including team management. Expect character sheet management, discussions about skill acquisition, and adventures. Wherever possible, we’ve put those in their own chapters. The final character sheets from the previous books have been duplicated as starting character sheets in this one.

Of note: Everyone but Jordan qualifies for Level 6 but has yet to claim it.

About the Character Sheets

We have a lot. Wherever possible, we’ve tried to avoid repetition or simplify mechanics. But, well, this is LitRPG. In our defense, we put most of it in stand-alone chapters. Also, if you notice some differences between character sheets in Book 2 versus Book 1, it’s because we’ve added clarification or updates based on reader feedback.

Recap

I awoke in the Fae Wilds with no memory, inheriting a Battle Scholar class from a fallen adventurer. After striking a deal with the Dungeon Core of the dungeon I’d woken up in, I rescued four ex-members of the Sisterhood: Merielle, a stoic and reliable elf warrior woman; Nym, an adorable catgirl battle mage and her spicy feline familiar, Sadie; a bratty celestial healer named Britnayel; and Zuri, an alluring half-medusa woman who happens to be an excellent cook. I also befriended Skullie, a snarky lich who became my familiar.

Because of my class, I was able to offer the ex-Sisterhood members temporary Tablets, giving them classes, and we formed the Society of the Defiant.

We traveled to Boulibar Bay, where we established ourselves by completing quests for the locals. Our party grew stronger, but tensions arose, particularly with Britnayel, who struggled to adapt to our new situation. We focused on gaining levels and improving our relationships with the town. Our efforts were interrupted by a sudden monster attack on the town, which we successfully repelled, pushing our abilities to the limit.

The mayor of Boulibar Bay, Mayor Regan, approached us with a secret quest to investigate disruptions to the town’s trade routes. Our resulting sea voyage revealed a smuggling operation involving powerful artifacts and elemental beings. We faced numerous challenges, including battles with sirens and a massive sea monster known as the Old Man of the Sea. Eventually we made it to Lans, where we learned of a larger conspiracy involving the Trade Regulation Bureau.

Returning to Boulibar Bay, we were tasked with investigating a suspected dungeon site in relation to that conspiracy. We discovered it was part of “Project Synthesis,” an attempt to create controlled dungeons using a combination of technology and magic. We battled through the corrupted facility, facing horrifying abominations and twisted artificial intelligence that had taken control.

In a climactic confrontation, we destroyed the dungeon’s artificial Dungeon Core, collapsing the facility and saving the region. Our victory earned us hero status in Boulibar Bay, along with a grand house from Mayor Regan. But our work has only just begun, as we still have to get stronger so we can take on a list of challenges, including a Demon Core beneath the Goblin King’s city, and a corrupt Celestial Queen (who happens to be Britney’s stepmother).

Prelude: A Gathering of Queens and Kings

The door to her private sanctum clicked shut, and Queen Leah activated the Aether Cage.

Static glowed along wires covering the circular walls of the relatively small, thirty-foot diameter room. The magic developed gradually, like salt crystals across twine. Next to her face, Leah’s Royal Tablet appeared, its golden light steady.

She watched for notifications or any other indication that the device, or its creators, was aware of what she did. There was no sign. Minutes crept by as, unblinking, the woman waited for the crystals to finish growing.

A subtle flicker ran across the smooth surface of the Tablet. Extending a delicate finger, she probed the surface. It did not respond. Sliding her gaze from the legendary artifact, she took in the room.

With the Aether Cage active, it appeared that she stood in a realm covered in rainbow-colored static, interrupted by thin vines of glowing rainbow crystal. Four mirrors decorated one wall, each barely larger than a Tablet. Appearing only once the Aether Cage was established, each mirror was wrapped in an elaborate wire mesh that seemed to blend into the static.

“I’m here,” she announced. “The room is secure.”

At her words, the mirrors darkened, replaced with the outline of four others. One held the silhouette of a dragon. Another held the distinct shape of King Vugnek, ruler of a local demon cohort. The final held what might have been an elven man wearing a robe.

Assuming her role as leader, Leah pointed at the dragon. “Great old one. Would you present us with your progress?”

“It is slow, but steady, Queen Leah.” The dragon had a deep, cultured accent. Humans from Earth might have labeled it ‘Proper English.’ “The information our scientists have extracted from the Dungeon Core has been priceless. Combined with imports from the Veil Tear, we should have working prototypes within the year. If all goes as planned, in a decade or two, we will have tools to rival the Fates.”

King Vugnek shifted, speaking in his bestial tongue. “I want one of those prototypes as soon as they are ready. The upstart Goblin King has become a bigger problem than any of us anticipated. He’s marrying the troll princess. My wife”—he said the word like a curse—“has even taken an interest in him. I want him removed.”

The elf said, “What about Reginald? I thought he was your proxy in that area.”

“He’s proven to be weak. If I am to continue with my part of the bargain, I need Thomas gone. If he continues to gain power, it may slow our plans.”

Leah waved to silence the debate. “Vugnek, you will be provided what resources we can safely offer. Remember, subtlety is of paramount importance, especially near the Concord Islands. We have reason to believe they are monitoring the area, which means teleportations are entirely out of the question.”

The implicit threat hung over all of them. If the Fates became directly involved, it could lead to Warden intervention, which none of them wanted.

She briefly considered whether to inform them of the so-called ‘Society of the Defiant’ and their recent success in destroying the research facility.

No, she decided. Why should I get my hands dirty? Betsy made the mess; she can clean it up.

“I think,” she said, adopting her most diplomatic voice, “we should discuss our contingency plan. If we’re close to having our own Tablets, we will need our own Wardens as well. I’ve been studying their dreams and found an excellent candidate. She’s on Earth, in the Las Vegas area.”
